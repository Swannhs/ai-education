Option (C): "Had he been a bird, he would have flown."

"Why it's right: This is a Third Conditional structure expressing an unreal present or future condition using 'Had' as an inversion of 'If'."

Concept Breakdown

In English grammar, conditional sentences describe the result of a certain condition. When we want to express a hypothetical or imaginary situation, we often use the subjunctive mood or specific conditional structures.

Standard Form If + Subject + had + Been
Inversion Form Had + Subject + Been
  • radio_button_checked The word "Had" at the beginning replaces "If", creating a more formal tone common in competitive exams like BCS.
  • radio_button_checked The result clause must use "would have + past participle" or "would + base verb" depending on the mixed conditional context.
